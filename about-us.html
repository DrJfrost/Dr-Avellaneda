Bio

I was born in Paz de Río, Boyacá, Colombia. I moved to Bogotá at age 18 looking for a better future, and opportunities to obtain higher education. After a year of struggling in Bogotá, I began my studies at Universidad Nacional de Colombia where I graduated with a degree in Philology and Language- English. After working at Centro Colombo-Americano for almost a decade, I moved to Hattiesburg, MS, to do my Master's in TESOL/Spanish. After graduation, I moved to CA to work in an immersion program and then to Miami, FL where I worked at Miami Dade College. I accepted a job a Spanish teacher at South Broward High School and combined my daily teacher with ESL classes for immigrants and refugees at night school in public high schools such as Nova and South Broward. I also taught at Miami Dade and Broward College. After 9 years of teaching, I decided to pursue my doctoral studies at the University of South Florida where I graduated in 2016 with a Ph.D. in Second Language Acquisition and Instructional Technology.

Education

For my undergraduate I attended National University of Colombia obtaining a degree in Philology in modern languages. I did my master, covered by a scholarship, in the University of Missisipi. Finally, I was able to complete doctorate in Philosophy in the Unversity of South Florida in 2016.
